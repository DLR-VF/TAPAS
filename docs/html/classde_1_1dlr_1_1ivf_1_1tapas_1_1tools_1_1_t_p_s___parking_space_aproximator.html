Theory behind it:

  1. Searching for a parking lot: The probability to find a parking lot in a area with a fixed availability of parking spaces increases linearly with the street length covered. The availability of parking lots decreases linearly with the density of cars is a specific region. ->Extension: We found this true for the first 90%! So interpolate between 0 and the 80%-Median So we interpolate between a given minimum (e. g. 10 m) and maximum search distance (e.g. 1500m). Additionally a search speed is needed to calculate the search time.
  1. Going back to your original destination If you found a parking lot, you have to walk back to your original destination. But the search-strategy is most probable a spiral around the original destination. We assume grid-blocks (Manhattan style). The search strategy is as follows: First round you drive around the block, called center block (4 Streets) Second round you drive around the cross around the center block (12 streets -draw it!) Third round you drive around the diamond arount the cros (20 streets) Forth round 28 Streets...

When you draw it you see that the length of the search line increases by eight segments every round (plus + street to the next circle, but we ommit that ;) )

The length of going back to the origin is the number of rounds but the length of the search distance is the sum of street segments for all rounds

So if you know the block distance, a walking speed and have a search length for finding a lot, you can calculate the walking distance between lot and destination.

Parameters
minSearchthe minimum search distance in meters eg 10m
maxSearchthe maximum search distance in meters e.g. 1500m
searchSpeedCarthe speed during the search in m/s (e.g. 14.4km/h = 4m/s)
blockDistancethe average block length in meters (e.g. 1000)
walkSpeedthe speed going back in m/s (e.g. 3.6km/h = 1m/s)
saturationFactoris the factor between 0 and 1, how many regions are affected by less than the maximum search radius.
